# Wheat Agronomy & Irrigation Guidelines

## Introduction
Wheat (*Triticum aestivum*) is a staple grain crop that requires careful water management, especially during reproductive phases. Yield is highly sensitive to moisture deficits from jointing through flowering.

## Soil and Moisture Requirements
- **Preferred Soil Type**: Silt loam or clay loam soils with good water-holding capacity.
- **Root Zone Depth**: Root depth can extend to 100-120 cm, but the primary water extraction zone is the top 60 cm.
- **Moisture Depletion Limit**: Irrigate when soil moisture depletion in the active root zone reaches 50% of available water capacity.

## Growth Stage Irrigation Management

### 1. Germination & Seedling Stage (Days 1–20)
- **Water Need**: Low to moderate.
- **Strategy**: Ensure adequate moisture in the top 10–15 cm for even germination. A pre-planting irrigation is often recommended if the soil profile is dry.
- **Risk**: Excessive watering causes waterlogging and poor seedling emergence.

### 2. Tillering & Crown Root Initiation (Days 21–40)
- **Water Need**: Moderate (2–3 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Crown Root Initiation (CRI) is a critical stage. Water stress at CRI (usually around 21 days after sowing) severely reduces tillering and yield potential.
- **Risk**: Missing irrigation during CRI leads to thin stands.

### 3. Jointing & Booting Stage (Days 41–75)
- **Water Need**: High (4–5 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Rapid vegetative growth and spike development occur here. Ensure moisture is available throughout the 0-60 cm profile.
- **Risk**: Moisture stress reduces the number of spikelets per head.

### 4. Flowering / Anthesis (Days 76–90)
- **Water Need**: Peak demand (5–6 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Maintain steady soil moisture. This is the most critical window. Avoid stress to maximize grain set.
- **Risk**: Drought stress at flowering leads to sterile florets and drastically reduced grain count.

### 5. Grain Filling & Maturity (Days 91–120+)
- **Water Need**: High to low (4 mm/day, dropping to zero as crop ripens).
- **Strategy**: Keep soil moist during the milk and soft-dough stages to ensure plump grains. Stop all irrigation 10-15 days before harvest (hard dough stage) to allow the grain to dry.
- **Risk**: Late-stage waterlogging causes lodging (falling over) and grain quality issues.
