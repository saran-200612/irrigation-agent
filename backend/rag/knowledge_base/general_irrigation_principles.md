# General Irrigation Principles & Soil Management

## Core Concepts of Soil Moisture
1. **Saturation**: All soil pore spaces are filled with water. Occurs after heavy rain or flooding. Leads to lack of oxygen for roots.
2. **Field Capacity (FC)**: The amount of water remaining in the soil after excess water has drained away due to gravity (usually 1-2 days after saturation).
3. **Permanent Wilting Point (PWP)**: The soil moisture level at which plants can no longer extract water and will wilt permanently if water is not added.
4. **Available Water Capacity (AWC)**: The range of water available to plants, calculated as:
   `AWC = Field Capacity - Permanent Wilting Point`
5. **Management Allowed Depletion (MAD)**: The percentage of AWC that is allowed to deplete before irrigation is recommended. Commonly set to 40% to 50% for agronomic crops.

## Soil Types and Water Retention
- **Sand / Sandy Loam**:
  - Low water holding capacity (FC ~ 10-15%, PWP ~ 3-5%).
  - High infiltration rate (water sinks fast).
  - Irrigation Strategy: Frequent, small-volume irrigations (light watering). High risk of nutrient leaching if overwatered.
- **Loam / Silt Loam**:
  - Medium to high water holding capacity (FC ~ 25-30%, PWP ~ 10-12%).
  - Excellent balance of air and water space.
  - Irrigation Strategy: Moderate frequency and volume. Ideal for most crops.
- **Clay / Clay Loam**:
  - High water holding capacity (FC ~ 35-40%, PWP ~ 18-20%).
  - Low infiltration rate (water absorption is slow, prone to runoff and pooling).
  - Irrigation Strategy: Low frequency, high volume. Apply slowly to prevent runoff.

## Weather Adjustments
- **Rainfall Offset**: Rainfall directly offsets irrigation needs. Effective rainfall is typically 70-80% of total precip, depending on runoff. If a storm is forecasted within 24-48 hours, irrigation should be delayed or skipped.
- **Evapotranspiration (ET)**: The combination of water evaporated from soil and transpired by plants. ET increases with:
  - Higher temperatures
  - Lower humidity
  - High winds
  - High solar radiation (sunny days)
- **Irrigation Efficiency**: No system is 100% efficient. Drip systems are 90% efficient, sprinklers 70-80%, flood irrigation 50-60%. Recommendations should adjust the total depth based on efficiency to achieve net crop water requirements.
