# Maize Agronomy & Irrigation Guidelines

## Introduction
Maize (*Zea mays*), or corn, has high water requirements and is very sensitive to drought stress, especially during the pollination and silking stages.

## Soil and Moisture Requirements
- **Preferred Soil Type**: Deep loams and silt loams with high organic matter and excellent water storage capacity.
- **Root Zone Depth**: Effective root zone depth is 60 to 90 cm, though mature roots can penetrate deeper.
- **Moisture Depletion Limit**: Irrigate when soil moisture depletion in the active root zone reaches 45-50% of available water capacity.

## Growth Stage Irrigation Management

### 1. Establishment & Early Vegetative (Days 1–35)
- **Water Need**: Low (2–3 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Keep the seedbed moist. Once emerged, minor moisture stress can actually encourage deeper root development.
- **Risk**: Standing water/flooding can stunt seedlings or kill the stand.

### 2. Rapid Growth & V12 to Tesseling (Days 36–60)
- **Water Need**: Moderate to high (4–6 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Water requirements increase rapidly as leaf area expands. Maintain adequate root-zone moisture to support leaf extension.
- **Risk**: Drought stress slows leaf growth, reducing solar interception.

### 3. Tasseling, Silking & Pollination (Days 61–75)
- **Water Need**: Peak demand (7–9 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: **Absolute critical stage.** Even 1-2 days of severe water stress during silking can reduce yields by 20% to 50%. Keep soil moisture above 60% of available water.
- **Risk**: Stress delays silking, which causes poor pollination (blank/partially filled ears) because pollen shed and silking become desynchronized.

### 4. Blister & Milk Stage (Days 76–95)
- **Water Need**: High (6–7 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Ensure consistent water to support kernel growth. Water stress here leads to kernel abortion (tip back), where kernels at the tip of the cob fail to develop.
- **Risk**: Inadequate water reduces kernel weight.

### 5. Dough, Dent & Physiological Maturity (Days 96–120+)
- **Water Need**: Decreasing (4 mm/day down to 1–2 mm/day).
- **Strategy**: Gradually reduce irrigation. Stop irrigation once the black layer forms at the base of the kernel, indicating physiological maturity.
- **Risk**: Late-stage excess water promotes ear rot, stalk rot, and delays harvest.
